Our commitment to better connections to Lakewood Station

Sounder trains and regional buses give Lakewood residents a comfortable and congestion-free travel option to Tacoma, Seattle, and other destinations. The improvements we’ve proposed in Lakewood are part of a larger effort to make it easier and more enjoyable for people to get to and from transit stations throughout the region. We want more people to join us on the journey!

[Quote about how someone might use the improvements]

The good news is that we, along with the City of Lakewood, are committed to making all these improvements to get to Lakewood Station and have the budget to do so. Each improvement will be implemented over time with a completion date of 2030.

Unfortunately, we won’t be able to construct all the improvements we’ve proposed, because of budget constraints. Based on what we’ve heard so far from the community and the City of Lakewood, we have a good idea on what we should prioritize. But we want to give you another opportunity to let us know what you think are the most important projects to build! Answer some survey questions below or email us at [email address] to have your voices heard.

Want more details about each of the improvements? Look at our Lakewood Improvements booklet!

More direct connections to Lakewood Station from 115th and Bridgeport Way

A more direct connection to the station for neighbors to the north and west

Right now, local residents can reach the station from the north via Kendrick Street (in blue on map) or via a long route on Bridgeport Way and Pacific Highway South (red). The new path at the end of 115th (black on the map below) will make this trip much faster and more convenient.
